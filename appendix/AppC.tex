We extract $H^{(1)}$ for the process $q\bar{q}\to Hg$ from
the one-loop virtual amplitude of Ref.~\cite{Ravindran:2002dc}
by verifying that its IR pole structure agrees with the universal
prediction of Eq.~\eqref{eq:HQ-expanded}, then identifying the
finite remainder with $2\,\text{Re}\!\left(\vec{C}^{(1)}\cdot
  \vec{C}^{(0)\dagger}\right)/|\vec{C}^{(0)}|^2$. 
The reason why we do this is because the $Z$ is that the virtual corrections are not publicly available, they need to be obtained from $\ee$, with an analytic continuation. To go about this differently we use the one-loop virtual corrections from
Ref.~\cite{Ravindran:2002dc} to find $H^{(1)}$ for the $q\bar{q}\to g + H$ process.

Expanding Eq.~\eqref{eq:HQ-expanded} in $\varepsilon$ for the
$q\bar{q}\to Zg$ process, the double-pole
coefficient is
\begin{equation}
  \label{eq:H-pole2}
  -\frac{1}{\varepsilon^2}(C_A+2C_F)\,,
\end{equation}
and the single-pole coefficient is
\begin{equation}
  \label{eq:H-pole1}
  \frac{1}{\varepsilon}\!\left(
  -\frac{11}{6}C_A-3C_F+\frac{n_f}{3}
  +(C_A-2C_F)\ln\frac{\mu_R^2}{Q_{12}^2}
  -C_A\ln\frac{\mu_R^2}{Q_{13}^2}
  -C_A\ln\frac{\mu_R^2}{Q_{23}^2}
  \right)\,.
\end{equation}

Now comparing with Ref.~\cite{Ravindran:2002dc}. The bare cross section at leading-order is
\begin{equation}
  s^2\frac{d^2\sigma_{q\bar{q}\to g H}^{(1)}}{dt\,du} =
  \pi\delta(s+t+u-M^2)\,

  \,,
\end{equation}

$G$ is an effective coupling where for the scenario we are considering we can take this to be $G\simeq\alpha_s(\mu_R)/4\pi$, and $\mu_0$ is the reference scale. \ABquestion{Remember there is a mistake in theirs

\subsection{Validation of \texorpdfstring{$h_{22}$}{h22}}
\label{ap:mathematica-h22}
