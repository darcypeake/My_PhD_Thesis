
\chapter{Symbolic coefficient validation}
\label{ap:symb-val}

The following figures present the key steps of the \textsc{Mathematica}
validation of $h_{22}$ and $h_{21}$ against $\mathcal{C}_1^{(2)}$ and
$\mathcal{C}_0^{(2)}$ respectively. The complete notebook is available
at Ref.~\cite{peake2026validation}.

\subsection{Validation of \texorpdfstring{$h_{22}$}{h22}}
\label{ap:mathematica-h22}

We validate $\mathcal{C}_1^{(2)}/8 = h_{22}$, which follows from
Eq.~\eqref{eq:coeff-convent} with $m=2$, $n=2$. Since $h_{10}$ and
$h_{12}$ were validated at NLO, the cross-term
$\mathcal{C}_1^{(1)}\cdot\mathcal{C}_{-1}^{(1)}$, corresponding to
$h_{10}\cdot h_{12}$ in the \textsc{ARES} cumulant expansion, is
subtracted before the comparison is made
(Fig.~\ref{fig:mathematica-C21-sub}). The output \texttt{0} in
Fig.~\ref{fig:mathematica-h22compare} confirms exact agreement.

\subsection{Validation of \texorpdfstring{$h_{21}$}{h21}}
\label{ap:mathematica-h21}

We validate $\mathcal{C}_0^{(2)}/(-4) = h_{21}$, which follows from
Eq.~\eqref{eq:coeff-convent} with $m=2$, $n=1$. As above, the
cross-term $\mathcal{C}_0^{(1)}\cdot\mathcal{C}_{-1}^{(1)}$,
corresponding to $h_{10}\cdot h_{11}$, is subtracted before the
comparison is made (Fig.~\ref{fig:mathematica-C20-sub}). The initial
comparison in Fig.~\ref{fig:mathematica-h21compare} leaves a residual
in $\gamma^{(1)}_\ell$, which does not vanish symbolically since the
SCET and \textsc{ARES} conventions for $\gamma^{(1)}$ differ. Substituting explicit values for the
$q\bar{q}\to Zg$ channel, the residual vanishes exactly. The other channels follow in
the same way.
